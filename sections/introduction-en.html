Introduction back to ToC

Despite the critical role of standards in the construction industry, their adoption is hindered by accessibility challenges, natural language complexity, and a lack of machine-readable formats. The BIM Landscape Standards Explorer initiative by the M&S committee aimed to address parts of these issues. The BIM Landscape Standards Explorer is connected to a carefully analysed and curated dataset of BIM standards and their metadata, shaped significantly over the past couple of years.

However, the data was structured in a relational database on one hand. On the other hand, the data was not machine-readable, not connected to other datasets, and not easily queryable despite being publicly available.

The MnS Ontology Aims To:

  • Provide a formal, semantic structure for BIM-related standards, although it can also be adapted for other domains.
  • Facilitate querying and exploration of BIM and related standards' metadata and relationships.
  • Promote interoperability and integration of standards with other datasets.
  • Establish a link between the metadata of standards and existing ontologies in the construction domain.
  • In the long term, the MnS Ontology aims to bridge the BIM Standards Landscape Explorer data and tool with the Built Environment Lookup Service (BE-OLS), which is also developed by the M&S committee.
